Creatine is not just for powerlifters. It supports cellular energy, cognitive health, and aging muscles—but misinformation still causes hesitation.

<Callout type="info" title="Science Snapshot">
Over 500 peer-reviewed studies support creatine monohydrate as safe for healthy adults when taken at recommended doses.
</Callout>

## What it does

- Replenishes adenosine triphosphate (ATP) for quick energy during high-intensity activity.
- Helps maintain lean muscle mass during weight loss or aging.
- Emerging research suggests benefits for brain health and mood regulation.

<Quote cite="Dr. Priya Desai, sports medicine">
"Creatine is one of the most researched ergogenic aids. The key is matching the dose to your body weight and hydration needs."
</Quote>

<Note>
Loading phases are optional. Most people see results with a steady 3–5 grams per day.
</Note>

<Callout type="success" title="Hydration Matters">
Drink an additional 16–20 ounces of water daily when supplementing with creatine to support kidney filtration.
</Callout>

Before starting, check with your healthcare provider—especially if you have kidney concerns or take medications that affect fluid balance.
